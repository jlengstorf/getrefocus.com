Who is it NOT for?

  • You’re simply looking for more information. In Refocus, we cover a lot of information on self-development, productivity, balance, and doing work that matters. But this isn’t a passive-learning experience (like listening to a podcast or reading a book). We want you to take what you learn and actually apply it into your life — that’s why we made Refocus an active-learning experience. There are exercises to do and worksheets to fill out. This can sometimes be challenging and takes work.
  • You’re not prepared to invest in yourself. Lots of people claim they want to improve their lives...but year after year, they stay the same. You need to be different. Everything we put into Refocus we learned because we were willing to invest in ourselves — both in time and in money. If you’re not prepared to invest 6 weeks of your time or the same amount of money it costs to spend ONE WEEK in college, then Refocus is not for you.
  • You’re unemployed. The reason for this is simple: If you’re unemployed, we don’t want you to add any unnecessary stress to your life. Investing in a program when you’re struggling to cover your basic needs is not a good decision. You need to get back on your feet first. The only exception is this: You have more than enough savings to invest in the course. Then you’re welcome to join.

Who is it for?

  • You’re considering making a big career / life move within the next year. If you want to do something different with your career or your life — even if you’re unclear on exactly what that should be or how to do it — then Refocus is a good fit for you. That’s because Refocus helps you figure out your direction. We dive deep into your life and help you uncover your most valuable skills, identify your biggest time-wasters and self-defeating tendencies, and show you how to put together a plan to make the change you need to make — even if you’re nervous to make it right now.
  • You want a system to follow — and are willing to play the long game. You know that change isn’t automatic and you’re not looking for a magic bullet. Instead, you know that if you work hard and do the heavy lifting up front, you’ll reap the benefits for the rest of your life.
  • You understand the difference between cost and value. Cost is how much money it takes to buy something. Value is the benefit you will gain after you use it. (Benefits like more money, time, energy, and confidence and less stress, indecision, self-sabotage, and overwhelm.) The $20 book that sits on your shelf is effectively worthless. The $20 book you used to make one positive change in your life is worth at least 10x what the book cost. If you agree with that, then Refocus is a good fit for you.
  • You’re willing to invest in yourself. When you hear that Nate took out a $1,200 loan when he was 20 years old to go to a fitness seminar…or that Jason turned down a stable job and salary to build a freelance web-design business from scratch, your first thought isn’t “That’s ridiculous.” Instead, your first thought is: “Interesting. I wonder what that led to.”

If this sounds like you... then we'd like to invite you to join.
